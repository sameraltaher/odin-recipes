Mujadara. Vegan Has Never Been this Wholesome Before.

Description

Mujadara is a healthy, inexpensive vegan dish that is popular in the Arab cuisines. Its base ingredients are rice and brown lentils. The star ingredient is the crispy fried onion; it makes the dish! The coriander which is added as a garnish also adds a blast of flavor.

We will be working with the basic Levant version of Mujadara, which, unlike the North-African versions (sometimes referred to as Kushari), excludes elbow pasta and any kind of tomato-based sauce. This recipe can be prepared with much ease and very quickly, so let's get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice (Basmati or any long-grain)
  • 1 cup brown or green lentils (do not use red or yellow lentils! They will not hold well.)
  • 3 large yellow onions
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 bunch coriander
  • 31⁄2 cups water
  • 1⁄2 tbsp sea salt
  • 1 tbsp high quality ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 45 minutes - 1 hour
  1. Rinse the lentils and rice with cold water (separately) and set aside.
  2. Heat 11⁄2 tbsp of the olive oil in a large skillet on medium heat.
  3. Cut off a quarter of one onion and dice. Set aside.
  4. Slice the onions into very thin crescent shaped slices and put them on the heated skillet.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low and turn the onions around every 5 minutes or so. Continue doing so for around 45 minutes until golden brown and crispy
  6. In a large pot, heat the rest of the olive oil on medium-high.
  7. Add the diced onions to the pot and brown them.
  8. Increase heat to high and add the lentils.
  9. Stir the lentils continuously for about 1 minute.
  10. Pour all the water and bring to the quick boil on high heat
  11. Once boiling, reduce to simmer, add the salt and cover. Leave covered for 20 minutes.
  12. Wash and rinse the coriander. De-stem and chop 1⁄2 bunch very finely, chop other half chop roughly and with lots of clumsiness.
  13. After 20 minutes, uncover the pot and taste. Add salt and pepper as needed.
  14. Add the rice and cumin to the pot of lentils.
  15. Bring to a quick boil then turn heat to low. Cover and simmer for another 20 minutes.
  16. After 15, check on the rice being. If all the water has been absorbed. Turn off heat and fluff gently with a fork.
  17. After 20 minutes, turn off heat. Fluff rice gently with a fork and spread on a long serving dish.
  18. Spread the fried onioins and roughly chopped coriander all over the rice.
  19. Place the fine coriander in a small sauce dish, along with any pickled goods, hot spices, or other condiments.
  20. Enjoy!
